Room Shifting


Within the facility, a security measure ensures the rooms and hallways are constantly shifting, making it nearly impossible to escape.
By default, this system works by the DM, either at chosen sections of gameplay, or at timed intervals, shuffling a series of numbered rooms to see what rooms are connected.
The DM can also chose to shift rooms on their own, to give players a helping hand on finding certain information or items, or to lead them towards further confrontation.

Clearance Levels


Every employee or visitor of Project Specularity and it's front, Clarity Shipping, are required to have an ID card at all times.
These ID cards are more than just a helpful identifier, but also give physical access to certain rooms and information, depending on the user's Clearance Level.
This level can be found in the bottom left corner of the picture on the ID card, and are ranked as follows.


  1. S+
  2. S
  3. A
  4. B
  5. C
  6. D

If a character's Clearance exceeds or is equivelant to that of what they are trying to access, they are granted access (excluding particular circumstances).
If the character's Clearance does not, then they are denied access.

Leveling Up


A character's Clearance can level up in a few ways:


  • Finding and merging a higher clearance card with their own.
  • Finding and using a clearance terminal containing a higher clearance.
  • Opening a higher clearance room from the inside.

Unless otherwise discussed with the DM during Character Creation, all players start at Clearance Level D

Dice Rolls


Rolls work much like DnD, in that the players (or DM) roll a d20, attempting an equal or higher number than that given by the DM.
Of course, the DM can roll for this clear number, make one up, or use a provided number, and can obviously decide whether or not to reveal this number to the players.
Rolls are also almost always accompanied by a stat or skill (see Character Creation for a list of stats and skills) that modify the final value.
With the exception of Sanity and Despair, any positive stat value increases the roll by that amount, and any negative value reduces the roll value.


Equation
x = R + S

x = Final Roll Value
R = Initial Roll Value
S = Stat Value

Sanity and Despair


When rolling Sanity, it's final value is decremented by the inverse of the character's Despair value.
For Example, if the player rolls a 16, and their Despair is +3, their roll is reduced to 13.


Equation
x = R + (D * -1)

x = Final Roll Value
R = Initial Roll Value
D = Despair Value

Items


Throughout the facility are many items and tools that may help or hinder the players. Some are simple tools such as construction equipment or an infra-red sensor.
However some are more complex, and require rolls or certain other items to be used, such as a Shard Projector or a Speclura Device.
Below is a list of items, their stats, and possible locations they're found in.

Items
  • Speclura Device

    This phone-like device allows minimal use of some low-class Shards, as well as having a built-in ID card and general phone functionality

    • Locations
    • Some Emergency Isolation Rooms
  • Shard Projector

    This railgun-esc device unlocks the full usage of any class of Shard, as well as allowing faster swapping of them.

  • Shift Reconfigurator

    This device forces the shift-points to reconfigure to the desired placements, allowing the players to counteract the facility's secure shifting.
    This comes at a positive and negative. On one hand, the players can navigate the facility much easier. On the other hand, it also allows the dangers to do the same.

Information


Shards


Shards is a term given to Speclura Panes, materials containing data, programs, and unique functionality.
There are two differing types of Shards, Regular Shards and Aberrant Shards, the latter of which has more powerful and unique abilities or functions.

S.A.I.A.


S.A.I.A is the facility's Speclura Artificial Intelligence Assistant, an AI that runs the facility.
Well, until it lost it's clearance level and memory archives. Now it acts as a vague guide for the players in the facility, trying to decipher what happened, and restore it's control.
