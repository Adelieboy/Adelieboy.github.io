Character Creation

This page discusses the guidelines for NPC and Player Character Creation, and as such is written a little shakily, as I'm still figuring out the rules myself.
Because of this, expect this page to change dramatically at random times, as I figure out what works best.

Aspects and Skills

Each player character and NPC has many different numbers that modify their rolls, either positively or negatively. The two that will likely come up most during regular gameplay, are Aspects and Skills.


Aspects refer to generic stats such as Strength (Str) or Intelligence (Int), and are used in much the same way as in DnD.
The values of these are asigned similarly to games such as Fallout, in that you assign a series of points (the default total being 8) to the chosen stats, with the addition of being able to remove a point from the default of 0. A single Aspect's value can not exceed 3, and cannot be any lower than -1. These values, unless stated otherwise by the DM, should not be modified after character creation, and do not level up.


Skills, however, refer to more specific stats, such as Medicine or Info Search. These are assigned based on the stat spread shown, and also are not modified, unless otherwise altered by an item or effect.

Wounds, Sanity and Dread

Wounds


Wounds are a measurement of sustained injuries, for example deep cuts or broken legs. They are used as the health of the character, where if a character reaches +5 or more wounds, they are knocked down. A knocked character can be restored to being playable if they are healed to +3 or below. If a character reaches +7 or higher, they are killed, permanantly removed from play. If a character has reached +7 wounds on the same turn they would have been knocked down, they get 1 turn to be healed to +5, or the death condition applies.
The type of wound matters, and can (as decided by the DM) affect certain rolls. For example, if a character has a Broken Leg, they may gain a Disadvantage Die to any Dexterity rolls.
Unless otherwise stated or discussed, all characters start at 0 wounds.

Sanity


Sanity describes the mental state of a character, starting at a default of +2. Sanity can be lost in a plethora of ways, from seeing something disgusting or horrifying, to coming across an otherworldly being. It can also be increased, albeit infrequently, such as by finding something calming or relaxing.
Sanity is about as important as Wounds, because if a Sanity score reaches -5 or below, it returns to 0, but applies a stack of Dread.
Sanity rolls are modified by reducing the final roll value by twice the character's Dread value.
