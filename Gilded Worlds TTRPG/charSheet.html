Strength


Used to check against any strength test, for example breaking down a door, lifting a heavy object, grappling, etc


Perception


Used to check against any perception test, for example spotting a sneaking enemy, hearing a gunshot in the distance, as well as checking the characters perception of the world, e.g. whether or not they break from a spell or effect that alters how they see objects or environments


Dexterity


Used to check against any dexterity test, for example juggling, lockpicking and stealing, chopsticks, movements in certain spaces. Can also be used as a pseudo-speed check.


Intelligence


Used to check against any intelligence test, for example coming up with solutions to a puzzle or predicament, knowledge in different subjects, understanding of complex ideas/machines/tech.


Charisma


Used to check against any charisma test, for example persuading/seducing/charming others, talking, looks in general.


Body


Used to check against any body check, acting as an alternative to most systems' Constitution and Size, for example whether or not the character can withstand a poison, if the character can fit in certain gaps, opposition to grapples or shoves.


HP (Derived)


Represents the characters current health status.
HP is derived from a combination of Strength and Body.
$HP = (Strength + Body) / 4$


Horror Module


Characters stats from the horror module are derived from the base module stat numbers.


Sanity


Sanity represents and is used in checks against the character's decaying mental state, as resultant of occurrences around them. Fails on sanity checks result in loss of sanity, and thus a higher difficulty on succeeding future sanity rolls. Sanity loss of certain amounts, or in particular situations can result in temporary or permanent effects and modifiers to the character.


Sanity is derived from a base 70%, with additions and subtractions from Intelligence and Perception, rounded down.



$Sanity = 70 - ((Intelligence-Perception)/3)$



Fear


Fear is used to check against the character's specific fears/phobias, and acts as a modifier to [[Character Sheet#Sanity|Sanity]] rolls. Failures on fear rolls may result in a [[Character Sheet#Sanity|san]] check, possibly at disadvantage.

Dread


Dread represents an environmental "vibe", for example a decrepit and worn down abandoned asylum would have higher dread ("spookier") than a sunny day in a park. This Stat acts as a modifier to [[Character Sheet#Sanity|Sanity]] rolls. In environments with higher dread, characters are less likely to succeed on [[Character Sheet#Fear|fear]]/[[Character Sheet#Sanity|sanity]] checks, but conversely are more alert and ready for action, and more likely to succeed on [[Character Sheet#Perception|perception]] checks.


Dread is derived directly from environmental factors, decided by the GM at any given moment.

Auram/Magic Module


If this module is used alongside the [[Character Sheet#Soulcraft Module|Soulcraft]] or [[Character Sheet#Soulspark Module|Soulspark]] modules, it should be noted that a character using Auram or any spellcrafting can not use a Soulcraft or Soulspark. In other words



A character with magic can not use Soulcrafts or Soulsparks



This rule can be ignored in favour of unique and interesting characters, and at the GM's discretion, obviously.

Soulcraft Module


Unlike several other modules, this module is more customisable per character, in that each character should have a unique Soulcraft, and as such the skills involved require much discussion with the GM running the game.
If this module is used alongside the [[Character Sheet#Auram/Magic Module|Auram Module]], there are a few restrictions to keep in mind, the main being that a Soulcraft can not be used/had alongside the usage of Auram, or any spellcrafting. In other words



A character with a Soulcraft can not use magic.



This rule can be ignored in favour of unique and interesting characters, and at the GM's discretion, obviously.
